01Kurum ve firmalara teşekkürTürkçe
Umman Sultanlığı İskan ve Şehircilik Bakanı Sayın Dr. Khalfan bin Saeed Al Shuaili başkanlığındaki 25 kişilik heyet, 30 Haziran – 7 Temmuz tarihleri arasında Türkiye’deydi. Ankara, Bodrum ve İstanbul’u kapsayan sekiz günlük programda yirmiyi aşkın toplantı ve saha ziyareti gerçekleştirildi. Bu kapsam ve içerikte bir ziyaret, iki ülke arasında daha önce yapılmamıştı. Ankara ayağında gerçekleştirilen dört sektör toplantısının ardından, Çevre, Şehircilik ve İklim Değişikliği Bakanımız Sayın [Murat Kurum] ile Umman İskan ve Şehircilik Bakanı Sayın Al Shuaili arasında bir mutabakat zaptı imzalandı. İstanbul’da ise Türkiye’nin önde gelen konut geliştiricileriyle bir araya gelinerek karşılıklı yatırım ve ortak proje geliştirme modelleri ele alındı. Yirmi yılı aşkın süredir Ortadoğu pazarlarında çalışıyorum. Böyle bir programın kurulabilmesi, o yıllarda biriken bölge bilgisinin ve Umman ile karşılıklı olarak inşa edilmiş güvenin sonucudur. Bu ziyaret, benim için o güvenin karşılığı oldu. Programın arkasında yoğun ve titiz bir ön hazırlık süreci var. SP Architects olarak Umman ile yıllardır sürdürdüğümüz ilişkinin bakanlık düzeyine taşınmasına aracılık etmiş olmak bizim için gurur verici oldu. Heyeti ağırlayan, kapılarını açan ve sahalarını gezdiren tüm kurum ve firmalara içtenlikle teşekkür ederim. 02Umman tarafına teşekkürİngilizce · +3 gün
Between 30 June and 7 July, a 25-member delegation from the Sultanate of Oman, led by H.E. Dr Khalfan bin Saeed Al Shuaili, Minister of Housing and Urban Planning, visited Türkiye for an eight-day programme across Ankara, Bodrum and Istanbul. The programme comprised more than twenty meetings and field visits. A visit of this scope and depth between our two countries had not been undertaken before. I have worked in Middle Eastern markets for more than twenty years. In that time I have come to know the region closely, and to hold a particular affection and trust for Oman. This visit was, in every sense, the return on that. What made the week work, however, was the delegation itself. Every member of the delegation — from the Ministry, from [Khazaen Economic City], from the [Oman Chamber of Commerce & Industry], and from Oman’s leading real estate developers — engaged with a level of attention, preparation and openness that I have rarely encountered on a programme of this kind. They asked precise technical questions, walked every site, and shared what they saw generously throughout the week. That level of participation is not a given on a ministerial visit, and it is the principal reason the programme achieved what it did. Following four sector meetings in Ankara, a Memorandum of Understanding was signed between H.E. Dr Al Shuaili and H.E. [Murat Kurum], Minister of Environment, Urbanisation and Climate Change. It establishes a clear framework for cooperation in urban planning, smart cities, green building, real estate finance and land administration. I have no doubt that it will translate into concrete work in a short space of time. My sincere thanks to: H.E. Dr Khalfan bin Saeed Al Shuaili and the [Ministry of Housing and Urban Planning] Mr [Badar Hamed Saif Al Shukaili], Director of Partnership & International Cooperation, who coordinated the programme from the Omani side with exceptional care Mr [Jamal Al Hadi], for communicating the visit with such precision and generosity throughout [Khazaen Economic City], the [Oman Chamber of Commerce & Industry], and every Omani developer who travelled with the delegation SP Architects has been building its relationship with Oman for many years. Seeing that relationship reach ministerial level has been a genuine source of pride for our team. I will be sharing the next steps very shortly.
03Devamlılık + Urban Octoberİngilizce · +1 hafta
5–8 October 2026 · Muscat Urban October — Urban, Home & Build Expo At the close of last week’s ministerial visit to Türkiye, H.E. Dr Khalfan bin Saeed Al Shuaili, Oman’s Minister of Housing and Urban Planning, extended an open invitation to Turkish developers, contractors and design practices: come to Muscat, see the projects on the ground, and take part. That invitation was carried by Anadolu Agency and by seven Turkish national newspapers. It now deserves an answer. Oman’s urban development programme has moved from strategy into procurement. The Greater Muscat Master Plan, the National Urban Strategy and new integrated residential masterplans across four governorates are all live. Vision 2040 is no longer a document; it is a pipeline. The Memorandum of Understanding signed in Ankara gives both sides a working framework. What it now needs is projects, partners and delivery. Oman is where this began. It is not where it ends. Over the coming months I will be sharing work from other markets across the region. The thread is the same in each: identifying a real opportunity early, and bringing it to the right people at the right moment. That is the work I have been doing for twenty years. From this year, I am doing it more openly. At SP Architects we are continuing the work this visit began, and we intend to be in Muscat in October. If Oman — or the wider region — is on your agenda, I would be glad to speak.
